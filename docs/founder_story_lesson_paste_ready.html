Part Two · Speaking

Now say it out loud

Writing your story is only half the job. Daniel walks onto planes and talks to strangers about KIND. He hands out bars. He never loses his ability to tell the story. Now you rehearse yours.

Step 1 · Read
Read your story to yourself
Read your woven story quietly. Underline any parts that feel stiff or don't sound like you. Change those words until it sounds natural.
Step 2 · Speak
Say it to a mirror
Look at yourself and speak your story. Where do you rush? Where do you go quiet? Those spots need the most practice. Slow down on the important moments.
Step 3 · Shrink it
The 60-second version
Now tell your whole story in just 60 seconds. This is your pitch. Which parts are truly essential? Cut everything else and say only what really matters.
Step 4 · Rehearse
Practise with expression
Speak with energy. Pause after big moments. Make eye contact. Remember — Daniel still hands out bars on planes. Your story should feel alive, not recited.
A tip from Daniel on pitching
"The most important thing is that a pitch is authentic and sincere. If people ask you a question where you have a vulnerability — be okay with showing it. If you have an answer to everything, people will worry that you're a know-it-all."
Part Three · The Interview

Get ready — these questions are coming

Below are the interview questions you'll be asked. Read each one carefully. Think about your answer using everything you've written today. You don't need to memorise a script — just know your story well enough that it flows naturally.

🌍
Opening question
"Tell me about yourself — who are you and where did you grow up?"
Start warm and personal. Just like Daniel opened with his father's story, open with something that says who you really are — not just facts.
💡
The spark
"Where did the idea for your snack bar come from? What was the moment you thought — I'm going to do this?"
This is your gap story. Be specific. Daniel said "I was skipping lunch and I couldn't find anything…" — a real moment is always more powerful than a general statement.
🌰
The product
"Tell me about your snack bar — what's in it and why is it different?"
Know your product inside out. Describe it with pride and passion — like Daniel described seeing every whole nut and fruit. Make me want to try it right now.
🛡️
The challenge
"What's been the hardest part of starting something, and what kept you going?"
Honesty is your superpower here. Daniel admitted he was terrified. Show your vulnerability — it doesn't make you look weak, it makes you look real and trustworthy.
⭐
Your values
"What does your brand stand for? What's the promise behind it?"
Answer with total clarity. Daniel could say it in one sentence: "Be kind to your body, your taste buds, and your world." What's yours?
🚀
The dream
"If this snack bar became as big as KIND one day — what would that mean to you?"
Let yourself be ambitious. But also tell me what you'd never want to lose along the way, however big it gets. That's what separates the great founders from the rest.
🎯
Wild card — be ready!
"Daniel said the difference between being nice and being kind is that being kind takes courage. How does that idea show up in your business?"
Take a breath. This is a thinking question — there's no single right answer. Connect it back to your product, your values, or your way of working. Think out loud if you need to.
