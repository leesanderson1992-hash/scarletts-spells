What Problem Do I Want to Solve?

Great businesses often begin with a problem, frustration, wish, or value. This lesson will help you notice what matters to you, who you could help, and why your idea might be worth building.

How to save this lesson: write in the boxes here, then use the main save button underneath the lesson in Scarlett's Spells so your writing is captured for review and spelling later if needed.
Lesson Aim

What founders really ask

By the end of this lesson, you should understand that many businesses begin with a problem, frustration, wish, or value.

A weak founder question: “What can I make?”

A stronger founder question: “What do I care about changing?”

Even better: “What problem do I notice, who could I help, and why does it matter to me?”

Big Idea

A business idea is stronger when it starts with a real problem

A problem does not have to be huge. It can be small, everyday, annoying, emotional, practical, or personal. The founder’s job is to notice problems other people ignore.

Deeper Reason

The “why do I care?” ladder

Example:
Problem: I want healthier treats that still taste exciting.

Why does this matter? Because lots of healthy snacks are boring.
Why does that matter? Because children may not want to eat them.
Why does that matter? Because parents end up buying sweets or chocolate bars.
Why does that matter? Because parents want children to enjoy treats but still feel good about the ingredients.
Why does that matter? Because food should make people happy without making parents feel guilty.
Type of Idea

Frustration, need, interest, or value?

A frustration

Something that annoys you. Example: “Protein bars taste fake.”

A need

Something people actually require. Example: “Parents need clearer allergy information.”

An interest

Something you love or enjoy. Example: “I love making beautiful desserts.”

A value

Something you believe is important. Example: “Food should use simple ingredients.”

My idea or problem Is it a frustration, need, interest or value?

Emotion

The feeling behind the problem

People buy food because of feelings, not just logic.

Some feelings

Happy, healthy, proud, safe, excited, loved, special, comforted.

More feelings

Energetic, less guilty, more organised, more generous, more confident.

Founder job

Notice not just what people buy, but how they want to feel when they buy it.

Decision

Pick your strongest problem

Score each problem from 1 to 5. The best first problem is usually one you care about, other people also have, you could build for, and people might pay for.

Founder Statement

Write your first founder statement

I noticed that __________.

I care about this because __________.

I want to help __________.

I could create __________.

I want customers to feel __________.

Example:
I noticed that lots of healthy snacks are boring or taste too plain. I care about this because children should be able to enjoy treats that parents still trust. I want to help families who want better snacks without losing the fun. I could create colourful chocolate date balls made with simple ingredients. I want customers to feel excited, happy and confident about what they are eating.
Speaking

Founder interview prep

An adult can interview you like you are a real founder. Use the questions below to practise speaking in full sentences.

  1. What problem did you notice?
  2. When did you first notice it?
  3. Why does it matter to you?
  4. Who else might have this problem?
  5. What do people currently do instead?
  6. What would a better solution look like?
  7. What kind of product could you create?
  8. What would make your product special?
  9. How do you want customers to feel?
  10. What is one thing you want to test next?

Lesson Conclusion

A founder is a problem detective

A great food business does not begin with: “I want to sell snacks.”

It begins with: “I noticed a problem. I care about it. Other people may care too. I can make something that helps.”

Not every problem becomes a business, but every strong business solves a problem, fulfils a desire, or creates a feeling people want.

A founder is a problem detective who turns care into creation.
