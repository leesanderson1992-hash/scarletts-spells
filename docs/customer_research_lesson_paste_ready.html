Customer Research

An entrepreneur does not guess what customers think. They ask, listen carefully, and notice patterns. Use this page to interview five possible customers and record what they really say.

Write inside this page, then use the main Scarlett's Spells save button underneath to submit everything properly for review.

Part 1 — Your Task
Get Ready

Interview five people who might be customers

Ask each person the same core questions. The aim is not to persuade them. The aim is to find out what they already buy, what they care about, and what might stop them from buying yours.

📝

Interview focus

Write down the product you are asking about and where you plan to find your five people.

—
Rule

Do not argue with their answers. The entrepreneur's job is to listen.

🎧

How to interview well

These reminders help you collect useful answers instead of trying to get people to agree with you.

Ask exactly what they already do Start with what they buy now, not what you hope they might buy later.
Let silence do some work If they pause, wait. People often give the most honest answer second.
Notice emotion If they sound excited, doubtful, confused, or uninterested, write that down too.
Part 2 — Five Interviews
Listen

Record what each person actually says

Use one interview card per person. Short answers are fine, as long as they are honest and clear.
