Write Your AI Research Prompt

Now write the prompt yourself

A good AI prompt tells the AI exactly what you want to find out. The better your question, the more useful the answer. Here's what your prompt needs to include — then write it yourself in the box below.

Your prompt needs to cover these things:
  • Tell the AI what your problem is, clearly and specifically
  • Ask it to search forums, Reddit, Facebook groups, and news articles
  • Ask who has this problem — age, type of person, how often it affects them
  • Ask what people currently buy or do instead
  • Ask what they wish existed — in their own words if possible
  • Ask for a summary of the key patterns it finds
Example of what a good prompt looks like

Search forums, Reddit, and Facebook groups about [your problem]. Tell me: who has this problem, how old they are, what they say in their own words, what they currently buy instead, and what they wish existed. Finish with a summary of the key patterns you noticed.

Write your own summary

Read the report. Close it. Then write what you learned in your own words — who you're going to help and what problems they face.

How to write a great summary

Don't copy from the report — read it, then write from memory. Ask yourself: who are these people, what frustrates them, and what do they wish existed? Aim for 4–6 clear sentences in your own voice.

Now use AI to check your thinking

Write a prompt asking the AI to summarise the report into one short paragraph. Compare it to your own — what did you miss? What did you get right?

Your summary prompt needs to ask for:
  • A single short paragraph (4–6 sentences)
  • Who the people are and what frustrates them
  • What they currently do and what they wish existed
  • A warm, clear style — written for a young entrepreneur

Part 4 — My Why
Go Deeper

The Why Ladder

Great founders know why they care. The Why Ladder helps you dig past the surface. Start with your problem and keep asking "why does this matter?" — five times.

Example

Problem: Healthy snacks are boring. Why? Children won't eat them. Why? Parents end up buying sweets instead. Why does that matter? Because parents want treats children enjoy but feel good about. Deeper reason: Food should make people happy without making parents feel guilty.

Part 5 — Founder Statement
Bring It Together

Write your first founder statement

Use everything you've discovered today. This is your first honest answer to the question: why am I doing this? It doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to be true.

Example

I noticed that healthy snacks are boring and children don't want to eat them. I care about this because children should enjoy treats that parents trust. I want to help families who want better snacks without losing the fun. I could create colourful chocolate date balls with simple ingredients. I want customers to feel excited, happy, and confident about what they're eating.
