# ADLE Child Experience Principles

## Purpose

This document owns the child-facing 7-UI product principles.

## Core Principle

High-energy interaction with low-stress feedback.

ADLE may use rich animation, sound, tactile interaction, missions, responsive scenes, and satisfying completion moments.

It must not create answer leakage, fake correctness, fake evidence, fake mastery, inappropriate scheduler effects, inappropriate reward events, punishment for first-exposure mistakes, or direct coupling between visual success and Word Treasure rewards.

## Modes

- Discover: teaching and noticing; answers may be visible when instruction requires it.
- Practise: guided manipulation; correctness may be local and low-stakes.
- Recall: independent production; answer visibility must be protected.
- Reflect: repair and memory work; teaching reveal is allowed, but retry must not be copy-from-screen.
- Probe: no-stakes diagnostic attempt; no shame, no score framing.

## Feedback Tone

Feedback should be specific, calm, and useful. It should avoid red punitive states, shame language, fake celebration for wrong answers, and mastery claims not backed by evidence.

## Reward Boundary

ADLE interaction satisfaction and Word Treasure reward economy remain separate systems.
